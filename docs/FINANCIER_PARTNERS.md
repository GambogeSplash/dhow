# Financier partners — the demand side

The single thing the precedent Ignyte winner (Dhahaby) had that Dhow doesn't:
**a named, licensed finance partner.** Dhahaby ran on Mawarid Finance (UAE) and
Al Jazeera Finance (Qatar) doing the actual lending while Dhahaby supplied the
tech. Dhow's model is the same shape — *marketplace, not balance-sheet lender* —
so a real partner isn't a nice-to-have, it's the proof the whole thesis turns on.

This is a target list and outreach kit. These are **leads to pursue**, not
relationships we have. One signed LOI or pilot before the deadline changes the
application from "plausible" to "happening."

## Why a financier says yes

We hand them something they can't source themselves: **de-risked, pre-verified
SME origination.** They see only borrowers whose creditworthiness is derived
from real settled payments Dhow processed — cashflow data no one can fabricate —
with the score posted on-chain (`scoreOf` / `isEligible`) so they verify rather
than trust. They fund the cashflow they can see; we take a fee. Capital-light
for us, zero-acquisition-cost origination for them.

## The regulatory fit (use this — it's a feature)

DIFC credit firms authorise as **Category 2** (USD 2M base capital), are
**prohibited from extending credit in AED**, and are restricted to
business-purpose lending. That is *exactly* Dhow's posture: we settle in **native
USDC, never AED**, only for **business trade**, and we **don't lend** — we
originate and the licensed partner funds. Dhow slots into the existing perimeter
instead of fighting it.

## What to ask for (in priority order)

1. **A pilot commitment** — "fund N Dhow-eligible importers on testnet terms,
   real terms once mainnet is audited." Strongest possible application line.
2. **A letter of intent** — non-binding, but a named DFSA-regulated firm saying
   "we'd fund origination from this." Most of the credibility, little of the lift.
3. **A design-partner intro call** — feedback + a name you can reference.
